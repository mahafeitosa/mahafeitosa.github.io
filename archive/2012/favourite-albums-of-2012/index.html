27 December 2012

Favourite albums of 2012

 

# 10 Soen ‐ Cognitive

Can’t say much about this album or band. Their music just took me at the very first listen.

Try Delenda

# 9 Of Monsters And Men ‐ My Head Is An Animal

Icelandic folk ballads quite difficult to stop listening to.
Try Mountain Sound

# 8 Jukka Poika ‐ Yhdestä puusta

Sunday mornings soundtrack. Warmth, beach, good memories… Jukka Poika.
Try Siideripissis

# 7 Lucy Rose ‐ Like I Used To

Oh! This album is nice. Very very nice. Every single song.
Try All I’ve Got

# 6 Sigur Rós ‐ Valtari

It is Sigur Rós. Enough said

# 5 Meshuggah ‐ Koloss

If you search in the Oxford dictionary for the definition of Thick, Bass or Heavy, you will see yourself looking at a little picture of this album, Koloss. Imagine a rain of sequoia trees, how would that sound like? Like this: Demiurge And Koloss’ artwork is one thing to appreciate.

# 4 The XX ‐ Coexist

. . .

# 3 Lamb Of God ‐ Resolution

What have they achieved in their seventh studio album? Another masterpiece, once again. Seven albums released, keeping the same style, and not getting boring or repetitive is not something that every band can do. Resolution is fierce. Like a Mudcrab. Favourite band in the genre.

Go for The Undertow

# 2 Stam1na ‐ Nocebo

I remember pre-ordering this album and waiting nearly two months for its arrival from Finland.break I can say that this was my most anticipated album of 2012. The photos and writings in the album’s sleeve are way cool, and the short documentary making of, shows the whole creative process of the band together with Joe Barresi producing.

Listen to Puolikas Ihminen
